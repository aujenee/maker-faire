Started in 2020, Antipodic is...

A dome-like structure, created as an isolated haven for endangered species. We focus on repopulation and trauma care for those that need it. As of now, we care for Koalas, Eastern Curlew, and Black-footed Tree-rats. Within our ventilated dome, they are given access to food, medicine, and habitat potential. We transfer these animals from dangerous situations in Australia and provide peak care before returning them to their homes.

Antipodic is an animal sanctuary located in Georgia which houses endangered species native to Australia. Demanded by recent tragic events, Antipodic was founded as a rehabilitation center and a haven for endangered species. It is our mission to treat those in need of help in an effort of contributing to repopulation.

Affected by the Australian fires and other factors of forced habitat loss, these species find solace in our non-profit.

Antipodic is our way of trying to change the world by taking care of these species and repopulating before releasing them into the wild when they're ready.

Antipode; n. Australia and New Zealand (used by inhabitants of the northern hemisphere).
